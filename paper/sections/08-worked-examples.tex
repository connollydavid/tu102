\section{Worked examples}
\label{sec:worked}

The table's consumers are decisions in a production inference stack; the
registration outcomes of \cref{sec:hypotheses-outcomes} carried the three
largest, and three smaller memos show the rows at work.

\emph{Attention-variant staging.} At decode shape, each staged element of
the key tile is reused once, so staging into shared memory pays a store,
a reload, and a synchronisation against the dequantisation it saves; with
the measured shared-memory ceiling the saving cannot reach the cost below
a reuse factor of about two, and the projection route (validated at
5\% on the staged pair) prices the alternative directly. The verdict
is shape-dependent and stated as such: staging loses at decode, can win
at prefill-like reuse, and must avoid \texttt{float4} tiles (the measured
split-conflict halves their bandwidth).

\emph{A serial merge step.} A 196-step dependent merge costs at most
$196 \times 299.9$~ns $= 58.8$~\textmu s if every step missed to DRAM and
31.7~\textmu s at L2 residency: against a ${\sim}50$~ms decode token,
at worst 0.1\%. ``Negligible'' is now a number with a stated worst case.

\emph{Streaming loads in a quantised matrix-vector kernel.} The policy
rows direct the lever precisely: the streamed operand takes \texttt{.cg}
(it never pollutes the L1), the resident weights keep the default policy,
and \texttt{.cs} on either does nothing the default does not.
